October 11th, 2020 Blog Post

Today, I finally hit the moment of adjustement to the college life through two weeks and got a routine. As many of you may know, college is hard. While it wasn't too difficult for me to get into the swing of things, I thought that I needed a rightfullty deserved break from the development space (mainly due to fear of burnout, tbh.) However, that decision proved well for me because now I can jump back into the swing of things comfortably.

Also during this time period, I've been blessed to be getting interviews and OA's for the upcoming SWE internship season. I've developed a love hate relationship with CodeSignal and HireVue, but I understand that in the current state of job applications, things must be this way. Not spoiling anything major yet, but some companies that took the oppurtunity reach out so far are: Roblox, Google, Robinhood, Microsoft, and SAS.

With the current state of Software Engineering Interviews, I'm glad that things such as leetcode are resources. A lot of people wouldn't consider my school (Oregon State University) to be the most "prestigious" outside of the Oregon/Washington area, so with leetcode, I can prove to the interviewers that I'm capable of doing FAANG level problem solving. Even a field such as finance doesn't have this "luxury." to even be considered for these Quant/HFT internships/new-grad jobs, it's very tough to break into the field without either a Masters in (related field here), or by being a Harvard graduate, so everyday I consider myself fortunate to be able to have a chance to prove myself.
